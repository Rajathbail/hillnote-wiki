Hexhunt

Version 0.1.0

Setup

Concept: Set in a post apocalyptic, terraformed world Two warriors fight to be the last one standing.

Players: 2-4 players

Required gear: Each player needs the following

  1. 50 card decks

  2. 4 Dice

Setup: Each player places 2 battery cards and draws 7 cards each

Gameplay

Charge

Roll 2 dice to determine the number of + and - charges you have

  1. For rolls between 1 to 5 you gain that many - charges

  2. For any roll that is 6 you gain a single + charge

Draw phase

  1. You draw a card

  2. Reset all of your attacks / defences on your side of the board

  3. Resolve any conditions or status effects you need to

Equip

  1. You may equip any number of cards from your hand to the board
    (A maximum of 5 cards can be equipped at any given point of time)

  2. You may upgrade any weapon cards you already have in play with a card in your hand
    (Form 1 - Form 2 or Form 2 - Form 3)

  3. You may discard any card equipped on your side of the board to empty the slot

Attack / defend

  1. You may consume charges to perform an attack

  2. Your opponent can defend any or all of these attacks with any defences they have in pla

  3. Once all the attacks resolve you calculate your opponents battery shields

  4. Some abilities may trigger combat automatically

Win condition

  1. You win when your opponent has no battery shields left

  2. You win when your opponent draws their last card

Trick cards

  1. You may play any trick cards you have in your hand at any time in the game

  2. Trick cards resolve immediately unless stated otherwise

  3. Trick cards stack when played in response, very specifically

    1. Any trick card played in response to a trick card resolves first.

    2. Any Trick card played after resolves after the Trick card played before it.

Conflicts

In this game, the text on the card always overrides any general rule. If there’s a conflict between a rule in the game defined here and what a card says, the card’s effect takes precedence. It’s the ultimate authority for how that specific card functions.

House rules

Customize Hexhunt to perfectly fit your group – create unique house rules and explore the ever-expanding world of new game variations and updates.

Have fun

Hexhunt is designed to be a game of strategy and calculated risk. Don’t take it too seriously. Laugh, enjoy the moments of tension, and celebrate your victories (and commiserate over your defeats!)
